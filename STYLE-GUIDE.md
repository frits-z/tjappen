# tjappen: Visual Identity & Photography Guide

This guide outlines the design principles of the **tjappen** digital pantry to help ensure future content and photography feel integrated and intentional.

## 1. Design DNA
The site follows a **Warm Minimalist** aesthetic. It’s structured and high-contrast, but the "Linen" base keeps it from feeling cold or clinical.

- **Primary Base:** "Linen" (`#fff3e8`) — A warm, paper-like cream.
- **Typography:** "Carbon Black" (`#1a1a1a`) — Softened black for high readability without the harshness of pure black.
- **Accent:** "Blaze Orange" (`#fe5a11`) — A single, punchy pop of energy used for highlights and interactions.
- **Secondary Tone:** "Warm Gray" (`#9e7f62`) — Used for subtle metadata and secondary labels to maintain the parchment-like feel.

---

## 2. Photography Direction
To match the site’s aesthetic, photography should feel **organic, high-quality, and warm**.

### Lighting & Tone
- **Warm & Natural:** Use soft, natural light. Avoid harsh flashes or cool-toned fluorescent lighting.
- **Soft Contrast:** Shadows should be present but soft. The goal is depth, not drama.
- **Color Temperature:** Aim for a slightly warm white balance to harmonize with the Linen (`#fff3e8`) background of the site.

### Composition & Framing
- **The "Hero" Crop (2:1):** Hero images are wide and cinematic. Always leave extra "breathing room" at the top and bottom of your shots, as the site will crop them into a wide strip.
- **The "Card" Crop (3:2):** Index cards are more standard. Centering your dish is usually best, as the site uses a `Center` crop anchor.
- **Angles:** Top-down (flat lay) or a classic 45-degree "diner's eye view" work best with our clean, geometric grid.

### Styling & Props
- **Minimalist Styling:** Let the food be the hero. Use clean, simple plates (white, stoneware, or matte black).
- **Textural Focus:** Since the UI is flat and text-heavy, photos should provide the "texture"—think steam, fresh herbs, or the glisten of a sauce.
- **Negative Space:** Don't crowd the frame. A bit of empty table or a simple napkin helps the photo breathe within the site's structured layout.

---

## 3. Interaction Principles
- **Subtle Motion:** The site uses micro-interactions (like the -12° icon rotation and the grayscale-to-color card hover).
- **Focus:** The "Step Focus" mode emphasizes clarity. In photography, this translates to a **shallow depth of field**—keeping the main dish sharp while the background blurs slightly.
