**Structured Warmth** is the photography standard for tjappen. It combines organic sensory appeal with minimalist structure to ensure visual consistency across the digital pantry.

## **1. Lighting & Tone**

* **Source:** Use natural, directional light to create depth.
* **Contrast:** Maintain soft shadows; avoid harsh flashes or high-drama black levels.
* **Temperature:** Apply a warm white balance to harmonize with the Linen (#fff3e8) UI base.

## **2. Framing & Composition**

* **Aspect ratios:** Always return a landscape aspect ratio photo (e.g. 3:2).
* **Angles:** Give preference to a 90-degree flat lay for geometric clarity or a 45-degree "diner’s eye view".
* **Focus:** Use a shallow depth of field to emphasize specific ingredients while softly blurring the background.

## **3. Styling & Props**

* **Crucial:** Stay true to the presentation of the food on the plate in the original photo. Never alter the way the food is presented on the plate in the original photo. 
* **Props:**Props should be minimal and complementary to the dish. Avoid using props that are too distracting or take away from the main subject.
* **Plating:** Use clean, non-reflective surfaces such as white porcelain, stoneware, or matte black ceramics. Playful or colorful stoneware plates are also great if they complement the dish. Avoid using them as the main subject.
* **Negative Space:** Ensure sufficient empty space (e.g., table or napkin) surrounds the dish to prevent the grid from feeling crowded.
